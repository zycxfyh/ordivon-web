01 / About

An umbrella, not a single product.

Ordivon began as an attempt to make epistemic governance executable: claims should remain distinct from evidence, confidence should not become authority, and important actions should leave durable receipts.

Those ideas are broader than one repository. They now inform other work, including FinHarness, where governance principles are applied to personal capital state, financial judgment, review, and simulated execution. The domain is intended to outlive any single implementation.

03 / Principles

A small set of hard boundaries.

01

Claims are not evidence

Every conclusion should preserve the source, transformation, and limits of what supports it.

02

Confidence is not authority

Persuasive output does not grant permission to decide, approve, publish, or execute.

03

Governance is executable

Policies should constrain real workflows, not exist only as prose beside the system.

04

Receipts outlive explanations

Important actions should leave durable evidence that can be reviewed after the conversation ends.
