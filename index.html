01 / Identity

A durable umbrella, not a single application.

Ordivon began as an explicit attempt to make epistemic and AI governance executable. The name now covers a broader body of work: systems that preserve evidence, expose uncertainty, limit authority, and make consequential action inspectable after the conversation ends.

Some projects study these boundaries directly. Others apply them inside a concrete domain. The common thread is not a software stack or product category; it is the discipline of separating what a system knows, what it recommends, what it is permitted to do, and what evidence it leaves behind.

02 / Principles

A small set of hard boundaries.

01

Claims are not evidence

Conclusions should preserve the sources, transformations, and limits that support them.

02

Confidence is not authority

Fluent or persuasive output does not grant permission to decide, publish, approve, or execute.

03

Governance must execute

Policies matter when they constrain real workflows, state transitions, and irreversible actions.

04

Receipts outlive explanations

Consequential work should leave durable evidence that can be inspected without trusting the original narrator.
