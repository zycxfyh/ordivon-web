01 / Purpose

Fluency is not proof.

AI can produce coherent language before it has earned trust. Ordivon is an attempt to make epistemic discipline executable: claims should retain their provenance, uncertainty should remain visible, and authority should be explicitly bounded.

The goal is not to make every system cautious by default. It is to make the path from observation to irreversible action inspectable, contestable, and governable.

02 / Principles

A small set of hard boundaries.

01

Claims are not evidence

Every conclusion should preserve the source, transformation, and limits of what supports it.

02

Confidence is not authority

Persuasive output does not grant permission to decide, approve, publish, or execute.

03

Governance is executable

Policies should constrain real workflows, not exist only as prose beside the system.

04

Receipts outlive explanations

Important actions should leave durable evidence that can be reviewed after the conversation ends.
