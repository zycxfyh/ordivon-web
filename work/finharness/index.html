Capital systems · Local-first · Active engineering

Make a material capital decision reviewable before and after the outcome.

FinHarness reconstructs capital evidence into local receipt-backed state, derives bounded observations and decision candidates, preserves human decide/defer/reject authority, and records simulated action and later review. Its purpose is not to predict wealth; it is to make capital judgment more visible, disciplined, and learnable.

Central question

Can a person judge a capital action from a state they can verify—and later learn from the process rather than only the return?

Most personal finance tools separate account data, portfolio exposure, research, proposed action, execution, and retrospective review. FinHarness explores whether these fragments can form one inspectable decision record without allowing an AI explanation, a readiness flag, or a simulated result to silently become execution authority.

The first product wedge is material decision review: establish current state, expose risk and missing evidence, compare bounded alternatives, record the human decision, and return later to compare expectation with outcome.

Current surface

The working system now has one canonical synthetic acceptance journey.

Capital input

Production import boundaries accept FinHarness-contract CSV and read-only Beancount evidence, then expose capital-truth readiness separately from evidence integrity.

Observation

Exposure, positions, timeline evidence, Daily Brief, and candidate scans make the current state and material concerns visible.

Human review

A read-only cockpit and a separate loopback-only review mode preserve explicit human decisions while reusing the same state and receipt locations across restarts.

Synthetic acceptance

task acceptance:capital-review proves canonical import, admitted truth, exposure, Daily Brief, candidate generation, review, blocked valuation, receipts, restart, and identity-preserving replay.

Evolution

The system moved from governance accumulation toward a usable capital-review path.

  1. Foundation
    Model capital truth, evidence, authority, and receipts.

    The repository built a broad domain and governance vocabulary to make consequential capital actions inspectable.

  2. Expansion
    Accumulate protocols, tests, proof surfaces, and workflow structure.

    The system gained strong components but increasingly risked measuring engineering maturity rather than completing a user decision.

  3. July 2026
    Compress governance and documentation around current owners.

    Duplicate inventories and high-friction gates were removed; current truth moved closer to code, Taskfile, routes, tests, and a few maintained documents.

  4. Current
    Prove and use the canonical material-decision journey.

    Further abstractions should improve actual capital review, recovery, and longitudinal learning rather than recreate deleted ceremony.

Open questions

The difficult work now lies in real use, retention, and outcome learning.

Can the first capital-review journey remain understandable for a user who does not know the internal ontology?

Which data and scenario inputs are necessary for a real concentration decision—and which add context without improving judgment?

Can scheduled retrospective review distinguish a good process with a bad outcome from a bad process with a good outcome?

What evidence would justify moving from simulation toward any narrower form of controlled external capability?
