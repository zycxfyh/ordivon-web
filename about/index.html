About

A durable umbrella for governed work.

Ordivon is an independent public laboratory maintained through open repositories. It connects systems, research, and authored notes while preserving the different maturity, evidence, and authority of each.

Origin

From one governance project to a broader discipline.

Ordivon began as an attempt to prevent AI-generated fluency from becoming unearned authority. Ordivon Core made that concern explicit through claims, evidence, execution frames, verification, debt classification, and receipts.

As the same ideas entered FinHarness and research work, the domain became more useful as an umbrella than as the name of one repository. The common concern is how humans and AI can build consequential systems without collapsing observation, judgment, authority, action, and responsibility into one opaque step.

Operating model

Separate the layers that create trust.

01Observe

Record the world or system state without silently turning it into a conclusion.

02Evidence

Preserve sources, transformations, quality, freshness, and uncertainty.

03Judge

Make assumptions, alternatives, risks, and counter-evidence inspectable.

04Authorize

Grant narrow permission through an explicit human or institutional boundary.

05Act and review

Leave receipts, reconcile outcomes, and keep responsibility visible.

Ownership and boundary

Public work does not imply institutional authority.

Ordivon is maintained by the GitHub user zycxfyh. It is not currently a company, employer, university, public authority, professional advisory service, financial institution, or production SaaS provider.

Repository tests, CI, reviews, and receipts increase inspectability; they do not make every claim correct or every project production-ready. Each project’s own documentation and repository history remain the canonical evidence for its current capabilities.
